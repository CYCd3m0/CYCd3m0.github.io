GCDxLCM

$$ m \times n = \text{gcd} (m,n) \times \text{lcm} (m,n) $$ $$ m = p_1^{a_1} \times p_2^{a_2} \times \cdots \times p_k^{a_k} $$ $$ n = p_1^{b_1} \times p_2^{b_2} \times \cdots \times p_k^{b_k} $$ $$ \text{gcd} (m,n) = p_1^{\text{min}(a_1,b_1)} \times \cdots \times p_k^{\text{min}(a_k,b_k)} $$ $$ \text{lcm} (m,n) = p_1^{\text{max}(a_1,b_1)} \times \cdots \times p_k^{\text{max}(a_k,b_k)} $$ $$ \because \text{min} (a_i,b_i) + \text{max} (a_i,b_i) = a_i + b_i $$ $$ \therefore \text{gcd} (m,n) \times \text{lcm} (m,n) = p_1^{a_1+b_1} \times \cdots \times p_k^{a_k+b_k} $$
